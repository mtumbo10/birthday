Puzzle 2: Codenames

Instructions!

Woo! Welcome to the second puzzle! This puzzle is based off of Codenames. I am unsure if you have played the traditional game before, so I can give a quick run-down here.

A normal game of Codenames is usually played between two teams: Red and Blue. Members of a team are split into a team leader and the rest of the group. To start the game, 25 codename tiles (see below) are placed on the playing table. The team leaders pull a gameplay card showing which game board tiles their team is trying to guess. This gameplay card looks like this:

Leaders give their teams one word hints and a # to help their teams pick the Blue/Red tiles based on the gameplay card. For example, if leaders need their team to guess "Mole", "Spot", and "Face", they might make their codeword "Dermatology - 3". The goal of the game is to get your team to guess as many tiles as possible in the shortest amount of time.

Obviously that is a not possible to play in this format. Instead, this puzzle is a shortened version of a game.

Your goal is to use the colorized list below to match the Red/Blue card and solve for the blank tiles on the gameboard below. Any remaining cards without matches will be the blank tiles. You should expect to see one remaining card in each column.

Reading left to right, you will be able to create a list of words. For example, if Texas, Chop, Elephant, Marbles, and Lion are the unmatched cards, this will be your list of words. The first letter of each word will create your "solution" to this part of the problem (e.g., in example above: T,C,E,M,L).
